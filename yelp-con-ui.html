Chef - A Yelp Conversational UI Bot

MY ROLE: Facilitator, Interaction Design, User Research, Personas, Wireframing, Usability Testing  ||  TEAM: Yuki Zhang, Roosevelt Faulkner, Ethan Helfrich  ||  DURATION: 2 Weeks  ||  TOOLS: Sketch

CHALLENGE

Yelp has 25 million average monthly mobile app users1. There are two types of people who use Yelp:

  1. People who know the specific restaurant they want to search for.
  2. People who have just an idea of what they want or a certain criteria (price point, cuisine), but not a specific restaurant in mind.

Our focus is on the latter group. For the second category of Yelp users, searching for a restaurant to fit their needs is time-consuming and labor intensive. They need to comb through rankings and reviews to come to a final decision. Our challenge is to leverage conversational UI to improve the user's experience when they are searching for something to eat.

Constraint: Optimize the design so the user is funneled down to one specific checkout option: delivery (as opposed to dine-in, take out).

Target Group: Millenials

PROCESS

  

RESEARCH

We interviewed university students and other millennials. To start, we broke our research into two categories:

  1. Behavior - how users search for food and make decisions about where to eat.

    To test behavior, we gave users scenarios of what they would do in certain situations when they want food (far from home, very hungry, new to a city, want Thai food, etc.). Some users showed us their search process on their mobile phones.

  2. Review structure - What elements of the review structure users are more or less receptive to, why or why not.

    To test strengths and weaknesses in the content architecture, we gave users scenarios and presented them with varied reviews, and assessed how these reviews led them to make a restaurant decision. Some factors we varied are:

    • Using both Google and Yelp reviews
    • Photos (high-quality, low-quality)/no photos
    • Short, medium, and longer length reviews
    • Positive, negative, and neutral reviews

    We also asked users to underline or circle the most helpful text in the reviews.

    An example of our research questionnaire. Users underlined or circled the text they found most hepful.
Research Findings:

BEHAVIOR

  • Trust: Multiple one-star reviews that rant about specific restaurant experiences weaken trust with Yelp. Instead, users visit other online review sites like Google, TripAdvisor, and FourSquare to find reviews.
  • Specificity: Users have difficulty finding restaurants based on a combination of specific attributes such as: ingredients used in dishes, dietary restrictions, price range, distance, etc.

CONTENT STRUCTURE

  • Photos: Bad-quality photos deter users from reivews.
  • Detail: Users scan for rich details and descriptions.
  • Text Size: Needs to be larger and clearer.
  • Stars: Users have a strong association with star icons - they are the first thing they glance at.
  • Number of Reviews: For users, more reviews = more credibility.
  • Higest Rating: Users mostly select the highest-rated restaurants to browse.

REDESIGN

Our goal is to reduce the number of texts the user sends to the bot. Our new iteration gives the user options to select preferences instead of typing them out. Presenting users options also allows them to explore preferences they might not have thought about in the first place.

We wanted to break away from the wordy text message structure, so we included buttons for users to interact with. Buttons allow users to quickly tap their selections as they scan through options.

What I Learned

1. Harness storytelling to showcase your design.

My team and I spent a good amount of time developing two personas that fit our design. We utilized those personas throughout our process. When it came time to document our final design, we again utilized one of our personas to tell a story. Contextualizing a design through a story is much more engaging to your audience, and demonstrates the power of your design in a real-world setting.

2. Know the technology you're working with.

We created a chatbot with the assumption that it can recognize and respond to any input. In reality, artifical intelligence is not advanced enough to do this. Knowing the current limitations of AI could have given us constraints to put on our design. We could have pinpointed where errors might occur in the conversation with the chatbot and optimized our design to address such situations.
